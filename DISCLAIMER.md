## This is not a rootkit

These tools exist for **legitimate diagnostics and compatibility fixes** on locked-down embedded Linux systems where traditional kernel debugging interfaces are unavailable. They are published openly, documented thoroughly, and designed with safety checks and dry-run defaults.

If you use them for something malicious, that is your problem, not ours.
